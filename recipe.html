Chocolate Cake

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • ½ cup cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 cup milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Mix all dry ingredients in a bowl.
  3. Add wet ingredients and stir until smooth.
  4. Pour into a greased pan and bake for 35 minutes.

Pasta primavera

Ingredients

  • 300 g (about 10 oz) spaghetti, fettuccine, or penne
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup bell peppers (mixed colors), sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup asparagus, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

Flavor Boost & Finish

  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese (or Pecorino)
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley or basil, chopped
  • 1,2 tbsp lemon juice or zest
  • Optional: ¼ cup white wine or vegetable broth for deglazing

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Mix all dry ingredients in a bowl.
  3. Add wet ingredients and stir until smooth.
  4. Pour into a greased pan and bake for 35 minutes.

Fish curry

Ingredients

  • 500 g (about 1 lb) fresh fish pieces (seer fish, pearl spot, or mackerel work well)
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1–2 sprigs curry leaves
  • 1 medium onion or shallots, sliced (optional, some Kottayam recipes skip onion)
  • 2–3 green chilies, slit
  • 1–2 tsp ginger, crushed
  • 1–2 tsp garlic, crushed
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • ½ tsp fenugreek seeds (uluva)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2–3 tbsp red chili powder (adjust for spice level)
  • 1½–2 tbsp coriander powder
  • ½–1 tsp black pepper powder (optional)
  • 1 cup water (adjust consistency)
  • 1–2 tbsp kudampuli (gambooge), soaked in ½ cup warm water
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh curry leaves (for garnish)
  • A drizzle of coconut oil (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Wash the kudampuli pieces and soak them in warm water for 15–20 minutes.
  2. Keep both the water and soaked kudampuli for later use.
  3. In a clay pot (manchatti) or heavy-bottomed pan, heat coconut oil over medium heat.
  4. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  5. Add fenugreek seeds and sauté for a few seconds until aromatic.
  6. Add curry leaves, green chilies, and (optional) sliced onions or shallots.
  7. Sauté until the onions turn light golden.
  8. Add crushed ginger and garlic, sauté until the raw smell goes away.
  9. Lower the heat to avoid burning the spices.
  10. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and black pepper powder (if using).
  11. Stir well for 30–40 seconds until fragrant.
  12. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and blend into the spices.
  13. Add the soaked kudampuli along with its water.
  14. Pour in about 1 cup of water (adjust for desired consistency).
  15. Bring to a gentle boil.
  16. Carefully place fish pieces into the curry.
  17. the pan gently instead of stirring to avoid breaking the fish.
  18. Cover and cook for 8–10 minutes until fish is cooked through.
  19. Turn off the heat. Add a drizzle of coconut oil and a few fresh curry leaves on top.
  20. Let the curry rest for at least 30 minutes before serving — this helps the flavors develop.
